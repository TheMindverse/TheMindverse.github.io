post-title: 720mg DXM Freebase + CBD + DPH

- **Date:** December 30th 2nd, 2023
- **Body Weight:** 107 Pounds
- **Dosage:** 720mg DXM Freebase, 50mg CBD Isolate, 100mg DPH

# Comeup

> I took the dph and cbd together and waited about 20 minutes before I dosed the dxm, about an hour later I started to feel the effects. Slightly numb and weightless as normal. The most dxm I've done in the past is 420mg (freebase), and usually its a slow build up until my vision starts to get doubled and I slowly get nauseous. This gives me time to lay in bed and pick some songs to listen to. This time it was not a slow build up, after I felt it start to kick in about 15 minutes later and it really hit hard all at once. Almost instantly I got very bad double vision and it was really hard to focus, at this point I went to lay in bed to try to relax and practice slow deep breaths.

# Approaching Peak

> It felt like my face and head were blistering hot, I couldn't feel my body at this point. It was a full body anesthetic-like numbness that I have never felt before on dxm; yet I could tell my head was on fire. Soon after I got that feeling in your stomach that pukes about to happen and you start producing spit, got up and puked into my puke bucket. At this point stuff was really starting to get intense, it was really hard for me to get back up to lay in bed so I just stayed on the floor because just looking around the room with my eyes open made me so dizzy and nauseous I knew I'd have to throw up again. I always come prepared with a puke bucket and an ice pack in my mini fridge incase I'm worried about my fever getting too bad.

# Peak

> I was laying on my side on the floor and closed my eyes, but I kept thinking I was laying in bed and I misremembered not getting back up to lay down. When I tried to open my eyes it felt like my body was teleporting back and forth from a 3rd person camera perspective to first person. I was looking at my body laying down and then realizing that was me. I was trying so hard to get back up just so I could lay in bed but I couldn't. This turned out to be a good thing as I knew I had to throw up again. I didn't feel nauseous so I'm not sure how I knew, but I watched my body throw up over the towel I had next to the puke bucket but I never felt myself throw up. It was a very strange feeling watching my body do something I could not feel at all or control. I wasn't sure if any of it was real or I was just thinking about doing it in my head and that was really freaking me out. I didn't feel it but I knew all my limbs were shaking super violently, my legs, arms, and my jaw were jittering uncontrollably and I couldn't relax my muscles. I most likely had serotonin syndrome at this point, but I never blacked out or felt like I was going to pass out so I knew it would get better once the drugs started to wear off.

> I remember throwing up at least two more times after that. I chugged water in between barf sessions because throwing up on an empty stomach hurts like hell, so it's better for me to be throwing up water vs pure stomach bile. At this point I gave up and laid on my side still on the floor, I have tripped dozens of times before and I have never seen visuals so bright and complex. I was traveling down a red 3D tunnel with geometric texture patterns melting past me as I floated down. I then entered a bright white room that looked like a sphere or a video game skybox. I vividly remember it looking like a "torus" with the slopes of the walls, and how there were these repeating fractal lines all along it. I honestly think my previous trips with acid influenced these visuals, like my imagination was going off of previous experiences and memories as it didn't have anything else to go off of; which is why they were so colorful and complex.

# Comedown

> The full 3D geometry did not last that long, even from a tripping perspective with all the time dilation going. That must have been the very peak, but after I throw up yet again in my bucket and I ran out of water. I wanted to get some more so I thought of going to the kitchen and refilling my glass, then a few moments later I realized I was actually in the kitchen doing that and I didn't just think about it. That was slightly unsettling. Once back I finally laid in bed and put on my headphones to listen to music and close my eyes. I was still shaking like crazy and I tried to hold my legs still with my hands but I just couldn't get it to stop. (Even 12 hours later my hands are very slightly still jittery and my jaw gets muscle spasms every once in awhile). I for the life of me could not enjoy the music, it sounded like it was super far away in 3D space from me, even turning up the volume was no help at all. It sounded awful, so I ended up giving up and laid on my side to wait it out.
